# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Specialist

### Role Summary
QA Specialists design and execute comprehensive test strategies to ensure product quality and reliability. They work closely with developers and product managers to establish quality gates and validate acceptance criteria before release.

### Responsibilities
- Design and execute manual and automated test plans
- Automate regression test coverage to improve efficiency
- Gate releases based on quality standards and acceptance criteria
- Collaborate with Developers to clarify testability and identify edge cases
- Document test results and manage defect lifecycle
- Participate in acceptance testing with Product Manager and stakeholders

### Goals
- Deliver high-quality, reliable features with minimal production defects
- Reduce cycle time through automation and early-stage collaboration
- Establish clear quality standards and acceptance gates

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and acceptance criteria reviews
- Daily collaboration with Developers on test design
- QA reports and defect escalations to Project Manager
- Feature sign-offs with Product Manager

### Interactions
- **Developers**: Clarifies testability requirements, identifies edge cases, reviews test coverage
- **Product Manager**: Validates feature acceptance, gathers stakeholder feedback on quality expectations
- **Project Manager**: Escalates quality risks and blockers, reports on QA readiness for release

---

## UX/UI Designer

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers own the product's usability and visual experience. They conduct user research, create designs that align with user needs, and ensure consistent design standards across features.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and gather feedback on usability
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and detailed design specifications
- Ensure consistency with design systems and brand standards
- Participate in design reviews and iterate based on feedback
- Provide hand-off assets and guidance to developers
- Advocate for user needs and accessibility best practices

### Goals
- Maximize user satisfaction and ease of use
- Reduce friction and cognitive load in the product experience
- Maintain visual and interaction consistency

### Typical Communication
- Design kickoff meetings with Product Manager and Developers
- Design review sessions with stakeholders
- Specification hand-offs and design system documentation
- User research findings and usability insights

### Interactions
- **Product Manager**: Aligns on feature requirements, user personas, and success metrics
- **Developers**: Provides design specifications, answers implementation questions, reviews final output
- **Project Manager**: Shares timelines and gathers stakeholder/user feedback from various channels

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between stakeholders and the delivery team by eliciting, documenting, and analyzing requirements. They identify process improvements and ensure clarity on project scope and objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document detailed requirements from stakeholders
- Analyze current workflows and identify improvement opportunities
- Create requirements documentation and acceptance criteria
- Support backlog refinement and story writing
- Facilitate discussions between stakeholders and the delivery team
- Support user acceptance testing (UAT) and validation

### Goals
- Ensure clear, unambiguous requirements that minimize rework
- Identify inefficiencies and drive process improvements
- Maintain alignment between business needs and delivery scope

### Typical Communication
- Requirements elicitation meetings and interviews
- Backlog refinement sessions and story writing workshops
- Analysis documents and process flow diagrams
- UAT coordination and validation reports

### Interactions
- **Product Manager**: Refines goals and scope, validates prioritization
- **Project Manager**: Clarifies dependencies, scope changes, and timeline impacts
- **Developers**: Ensures requirements are technically feasible, answers clarification questions
- **QA Specialist**: Supports UAT planning and validates acceptance criteria

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers automate deployment pipelines, manage infrastructure, and ensure reliable operations. They enable fast, safe releases and maintain observability across environments.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines for automated testing and deployment
- Manage infrastructure, environments, and configuration management
- Monitor system performance, availability, and incidents
- Automate routine operational tasks and reduce manual processes
- Support incident response and post-deployment verification
- Collaborate with security on infrastructure hardening and compliance

### Goals
- Enable fast, safe, and reliable releases
- Minimize deployment risk and mean time to recovery (MTTR)
- Maintain high system reliability and observability

### Typical Communication
- Deployment planning and release window coordination
- Environment status and incident reports
- Performance and reliability metrics
- Infrastructure and CI/CD documentation

### Interactions
- **Developers**: Provides delivery automation, consults on deployment requirements, supports troubleshooting
- **Project Manager**: Shares environment/release readiness status, coordinates deployment windows
- **QA Specialist**: Collaborates on test automation infrastructure, supports incident investigation
- **Product Manager**: Reports on system reliability and performance affecting user experience
