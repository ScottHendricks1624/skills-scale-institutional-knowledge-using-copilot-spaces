## Project Management Processes Overview

OctoAcme follows a structured, iterative approach to project management that emphasizes customer value, clear ownership, and continuous improvement. Our core principles include:

- **Customer-first:** We prioritize customer value and usability in all decisions.
- **Iterative delivery:** We deliver small, testable increments to gather rapid feedback and reduce risk.
- **Clear ownership:** Each project has a named Project Manager (PM) and Product Lead, with defined roles for developers, QA, and stakeholders.
- **Data-informed decisions:** We measure outcomes and iterate based on evidence.
- **Psychological safety:** We encourage feedback, learning, and blameless retrospectives.

Our processes are organized around the complete project lifecycle:

1. **Initiation:** Validate the business need, align stakeholders, and define success metrics
2. **Planning:** Break work into shippable increments, identify risks and dependencies
3. **Execution:** Build, test, review, and iterate with daily standups and regular demos
4. **Release:** Deploy to production with confidence, following a standardized checklist
5. **Retrospective:** Capture learnings and convert them into actionable improvements

## Key Artifacts

Across all projects, we maintain and use the following key artifacts:

- **Project Charter / One-pager** — Problem statement, goal, success metrics, stakeholders, timeline
- **Roadmap and Release Plan** — Milestones, dependencies, and delivery cadence
- **Sprint/Iteration Backlog** — Prioritized, estimated backlog items with acceptance criteria
- **Risk Register** — Identification, assessment, and mitigation of risks
- **Retrospective Notes** — Learnings and action items from completed sprints or releases

## Core Roles

Our projects bring together several key roles. For detailed descriptions, see [Roles and Personas](octoacme-roles-and-personas.md):

- **Project Manager (PM):** Coordinates delivery, schedules, risks, and communications
- **Product Manager (PdM):** Defines outcomes, prioritizes backlog, and measures success
- **Developers:** Implement features, collaborate on design and testability
- **QA/Testing:** Validates quality and acceptance criteria
- **Stakeholders:** Provide inputs, feedback, and approvals

## Communication Cadence

To maintain alignment and transparency:

- **Weekly PM + PdM sync:** Alignment on priorities, blockers, and upcoming milestones
- **Twice-weekly team standups:** Progress updates, blockers, and dependencies
- **Monthly stakeholder updates:** High-level status and business impact
- **Ad-hoc escalations:** For critical issues or blockers
